About

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. student in Statistics at North Carolina State University (NCSU), co-advised by Dr. Wenbin Lu and Dr. Rui Song. Prior to that, I obtained a B.S. in Statistics from Zhejiang University in July 2017.

In the era of causal evolution with the new generation of artificial intelligence, it is crucial to establish reliable, powerful, and interpretable solutions to scientific questions. Toward these goals, I possess broad interests in methodology and theory in causal inference, reinforcement learning, graphical model, and their interchanges. My recent research includes individualized optimal decision making with complex data, policy evaluation in reinforcement/deep learning, and causal discovery with high-dimensional individual mediation analysis. The motivation behind my work stems from wide real-world applications in precision medicine, customized economics, personalized marketing, modern epidemiology, etc.
